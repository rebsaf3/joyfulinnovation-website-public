Review Software Inventory: The Backbone of Effective SAM

Published February 15, 2026 • 6 minute read

Opening Reflection

A well-maintained software inventory is the backbone of effective Software Asset Management (SAM). Without accurate data, organizations risk compliance issues, overspending, and operational inefficiencies. This is why reviewing and validating your software inventory is not just a best practice—it’s a necessity.

Connection to the Article

The ServiceNow Community article, “Review Software Inventory,” emphasizes the critical role of inventory accuracy in determining license compliance. It explains how SAM Pro uses the Configuration Management Database (CMDB) to track software installations and their attributes—such as publisher, product, version, and edition. The article also highlights the normalization process, which aligns discovered data with ServiceNow’s software library to ensure consistency and accuracy. Additionally, it underscores the importance of understanding the infrastructure context—whether the software is deployed on-premises, in clusters, or in the cloud—since licensing requirements often vary by environment.

My Real Perspective

From my experience, the three key steps—comprehensive discovery, attribute validation, and infrastructure mapping—are essential for avoiding compliance pitfalls. Missing details like edition or version can lead to incorrect license positions, which in turn can result in costly true-ups or audits. The normalization process is a game changer because it standardizes data and helps reconcile entitlements with actual usage. However, when gaps occur, manual intervention and collaboration with discovery teams become critical to maintain data integrity.

Takeaway

A robust software inventory is more than a list—it’s a strategic asset that drives compliance, cost optimization, and informed decision-making. Organizations that invest in accurate discovery and normalization processes position themselves to reduce risk and maximize value. The question is: How confident are you in the accuracy of your current software inventory?

Key Takeaway

Inventory accuracy plus normalization is what makes license positions defensible and optimization repeatable.
