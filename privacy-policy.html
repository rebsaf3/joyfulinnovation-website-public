Privacy Policy for Joyful Innovation

Effective date: February 15, 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how Joyful Innovation (“Joyful Innovation,” “we,” “us,” “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects information when you visit our website or use our products and services (collectively, the “Services”).

1. Scope

This Policy applies to information collected through:

  • Our website and related pages
  • Our products, apps, and services
  • Sales, support, marketing, and other communications

If a third party service is linked from our site or integrated into our Services, that third party’s privacy practices apply to their services, not ours.

2. Information we collect

We collect information in three main ways:

A. Information you provide

Examples include:

  • Contact information such as name, email address, phone number, company name, and job title
  • Messages you send through forms, chat, email, or support requests
  • Account information if you create an account
  • Payment details if you purchase paid services (payments are processed by third party payment processors, and we typically do not store full payment card numbers)
  • Customer Content such as files, text, prompts, instructions, or other materials you upload or submit through the Services

B. Information collected automatically

When you use the Services, we may automatically collect:

  • Device and browser information such as IP address, browser type, device identifiers, operating system, and approximate location derived from IP
  • Usage data such as pages viewed, links clicked, time spent, and referring and exit pages
  • Log data for security and troubleshooting

C. Cookies and similar technologies

We may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to:

  • Operate and secure the site
  • Remember preferences
  • Measure traffic and performance
  • Support marketing and analytics

You can control cookies through your browser settings. If you disable cookies, some parts of the Services may not function properly.

3. How we use information

We use information to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the Services
  • Process transactions and deliver purchased services
  • Respond to inquiries and provide customer support
  • Send service related communications such as confirmations and important updates
  • Improve the Services, including performance and user experience
  • Monitor for security, fraud, abuse, and violations of our Terms
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our rights
  • With your consent, send marketing communications

4. AI and automated processing

Some Services may use automated systems, including AI models, to process Customer Content and generate outputs.

If you use features that involve AI or automation:

  • Your inputs may be processed to generate outputs and to operate the feature
  • You are responsible for not submitting sensitive personal data unless the feature is designed and approved for that purpose
  • Outputs may be inaccurate, incomplete, or inappropriate, and you must review outputs before relying on them

We do not use Customer Content for advertising targeting.

5. How we share information

We may share information in the following situations:

A. Service providers and processors

We share information with vendors that help us operate the Services, such as:

  • Hosting and infrastructure providers
  • Analytics providers
  • Customer support tooling
  • Payment processors
  • Email and communication providers

These vendors are allowed to use information only to provide services to us, subject to contractual obligations.

B. Business transfers

If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, reorganization, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

C. Legal and safety

We may disclose information if we believe it is necessary to:

  • Comply with law, regulation, legal process, or government requests
  • Protect the rights, safety, and security of Joyful Innovation, our users, or the public
  • Prevent fraud, abuse, or security incidents
  • Enforce our Terms

D. With your direction

We may share information when you request or authorize it, such as when you connect third party integrations.

6. Data retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary to:

  • Provide the Services
  • Meet legal, accounting, or reporting obligations
  • Resolve disputes
  • Enforce our agreements

Retention periods can vary depending on the type of information and how it is used. If you request deletion, we will honor it where required by law and where technically feasible, subject to lawful exceptions.

7. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards to protect information. However, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Your rights and choices

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your personal information
  • Object to or restrict certain processing
  • Request portability of your information
  • Opt out of marketing emails

To opt out of marketing, use the unsubscribe link in the email or contact us. Service related messages may still be sent even if you opt out of marketing.

9. U.S. state privacy disclosures

If you live in a U.S. state with privacy laws (such as California, Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, or Utah), you may have additional rights. We do not sell personal information as that term is defined under these laws. We also do not knowingly share personal information for cross context behavioral advertising.

If you would like to exercise applicable rights, contact us using the information in the Contact section. We may need to verify your request before fulfilling it.

10. International visitors

If you access the Services from outside the United States, you understand that information may be transferred to and processed in the United States and other countries where we or our vendors operate. These locations may have different data protection laws than your jurisdiction.

11. Children’s privacy

The Services are not intended for children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, contact us and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

12. Third party links

The Services may contain links to third party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those third parties. Review their policies before providing information.

13. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. The Effective date above reflects when the current version starts. Continued use of the Services after changes means you accept the updated Policy.
