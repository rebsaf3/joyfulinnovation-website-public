How NyLi Insights works (typical flow)

1. Bring data together

Configure data ingestion from relevant sources so key metrics (sales, marketing, engagement, operations) can be analyzed in one place.

Use dashboards and visual reports so teams can review KPIs and trends without stitching spreadsheets manually.

3. Ask questions in plain language

Query data using natural language, like: “How are our sales trending this quarter?” or “Which product is performing best and why?”

Receive AI-generated narrative summaries and alert-style updates when key metrics shift.

Frequently asked questions

What data sources can NyLi Insights connect to?

NyLi Insights is designed to work with the data platforms your team already uses. Common sources include CRM exports, spreadsheet-based operational data, marketing analytics outputs, and business intelligence feeds. We scope specific integrations during discovery.

Do we need a data team to use NyLi Insights?

No. NyLi Insights is built for business users. The natural-language query interface means anyone can ask questions and explore data without writing SQL or depending on a dedicated analyst.

How are dashboards built and maintained?

Dashboards are configured during the implementation engagement. Once set up, business users can view and interact with them without technical support. We also advise on update and review cadences as part of our enablement plan.

What are AI-generated narrative summaries?

NyLi Insights can generate plain-English summaries that describe what changed in your data, why it might matter, and what it could mean for upcoming decisions. This makes reporting accessible to non-technical leadership and reduces interpretation time.

How long does a NyLi Insights implementation take?

A focused implementation for one reporting area typically runs 4–10 weeks depending on data complexity and integration scope. We conduct a discovery session first to define the right phasing for your team.

Can we start with one team and expand later?

Yes. We recommend starting with the highest-priority reporting need, getting it right, and expanding from there. This phased approach results in faster adoption and stronger governance across the organization.
