Reflections on Software Asset Management On Demand

Published February 15, 2026 • 6 minute read

Opening Reflection

In my professional journey, I've consistently seen how managing software assets is not just about compliance—it's about unlocking real business value. The challenge is always the same: how do we reduce risk and cost, while making sure our teams have the tools they need to succeed?

Connection to the Article

The ServiceNow blog post, "Software Asset Management: On Demand," lays out a practical roadmap for anyone looking to take control of their software assets. It breaks the SAM journey into four clear phases: onboarding, activation, deployment, and usage. Each phase is supported by resources and event recordings, making it easier to navigate the complexities of software asset management. The article emphasizes the importance of starting with a solid foundation, using the right plugins and store apps, and optimizing SaaS spend. It also highlights the critical role of compliance—especially when it comes to licensing for products like SAP, Microsoft, and Oracle. The message is clear: with the right approach, SAM can help organizations reduce compliance risk and lower software costs.

My Real Perspective

From what I've learned and observed, the structured approach described in the article resonates with best practices I've seen work in real organizations. The focus on onboarding and activation ensures that teams are set up for success from the start. SaaS optimization is a game changer—I've seen firsthand how quickly savings can be realized when organizations get serious about managing their SaaS spend. The emphasis on compliance is also crucial; taking inventory and understanding your license position is the only way to avoid costly surprises. What stands out to me is the value of having a centralized hub for SAM resources, which streamlines the journey and supports both newcomers and seasoned professionals.

Takeaway

Software asset management isn't just a technical process—it's a strategic discipline that can transform how organizations operate. By following a structured journey and leveraging the right tools and resources, teams can reduce risk, optimize costs, and ensure compliance. The real challenge is committing to the process and making SAM a core part of your business strategy. What steps are you taking today to get your software assets under control?

Key Takeaway

When SAM is treated as a disciplined journey, teams reduce surprises, improve audit confidence, and create repeatable cost-optimization habits.
